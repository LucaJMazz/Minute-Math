Answer Formatting Guide

Minute Math

General Rules

Answers must be typed exactly in the form requested. Minute Math uses a basic math parser that expects specific formats to automatically check correctness.

Decimals

Minute Math will never have a decimal answer, always use exact values.
Leave your answer as a fraction, ratio or as a function of a number (i.e. sin(5))

Fractions

Use forward slashes: 3/4, -5/2
If you have multiple terms in your fraction, use brackets to differentiate each term at the top and bottom
I.e. (x+1)/(2x-5)

Exponents

Use caret (^) notation: x^2, e^x
Use brackets to denote multiple terms or values in a single exponent
I.e. e^(2x), x^(-1/2)

Logarithms

Use ln(x) for natural log and log(x) for base 10 log

Multiple Answers

Separate answers with commas: 3,4 or (x,y)=(2,5),(3,6) for systems
If solving for x with multiple possible values, separate each by a comma
I.e. x=-3,3

Simplifying

If asked to simplify an expression, your answer must be in its simplest form
