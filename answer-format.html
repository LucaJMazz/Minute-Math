Answer Formatting Guide

Minute Math

General Rules

All answers are entered using LaTeX-style formatting and render automatically as you type. Answers must match the requested format exactly.

Keyboard Button

The KB button opens a list of supported mathematical symbols and functions. Clicking one inserts the correct LaTeX into the input box automatically.

Exact Values

Decimal answers are never required unless explicitly stated. Leave your answers as exact fractions, radicals, constants, or functions such as sin(5) or pi/4.

Fractions

Use forward slashes like 3/4 and -5/2. They automatically render as formatted fractions while typing.

Exponents

Use the caret ^ for exponents. Examples: x^2, e^x, e^(2x), x^(-1/2). You do not need to include brackets in exponents.

Logarithms

Use ln(x) for natural logarithms and log(x) for base-10 logarithms.

Multiple Answers

Separate multiple solutions with commas. Examples: x=-3,3 and (2,5),(3,6).

Simplifying

Unless instructed otherwise, answers should be left in simplest exact form with reduced fractions and simplified expressions.
